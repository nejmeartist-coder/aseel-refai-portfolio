Business Challenge

Healthcare distribution operations teams in the Dallas–Fort Worth market often need new hires to ramp quickly across order flow, inventory coordination, documentation standards, customer communication, exception handling, escalation paths, and system navigation. For new Customer Experience and Supply Chain Operations employees, onboarding can become inconsistent when training depends heavily on shadowing, scattered SOPs, and manager-by-manager knowledge transfer. This portfolio case study demonstrates how a structured 30-60-90 day onboarding curriculum can standardize ramp-up, improve process understanding, increase documentation accuracy, and give managers clearer visibility into learner readiness.

Inconsistent onboarding

Shadowing and manager-led training can vary by team, creating uneven new-hire readiness.

Complex operational workflows

New hires must understand order lifecycle, inventory issues, customer communication, and escalation paths quickly.

Documentation & brand alignment & quality sensitivity

Healthcare distribution roles require accurate records, clear handoffs, and careful documentation habits.

Limited readiness visibility

Managers need better ways to track progress, knowledge checks, overdue training, and 30/60/90 day readiness.

Learning Goals

By the end of the onboarding program, learners will be able to:

Objective 01

Explain the digital retail operations order lifecycle from order intake through fulfillment, delivery, exception handling, and customer follow-up.

Objective 02

Identify common order, inventory, documentation, access, and customer communication exceptions.

Objective 03

Apply escalation paths for delayed orders, missing documentation, inventory discrepancies, system access issues, and urgent customer requests.

Objective 04

Complete operational documentation accurately using SOPs, job aids, checklists, and role-based quality standards.

Objective 05

Demonstrate onboarding readiness through scenario practice, knowledge checks, manager observation, LMS completion tracking, and 30/60/90 day check-ins.

Instructional Design Process — ADDIE

The program was built using the ADDIE framework, grounded in adult learning principles: relevance to job tasks, scenario-based practice, feedback, spaced learning, manager reinforcement, and performance support at the moment of need.

1

Analyze

Reviewed target learner roles, onboarding pain points, digital retail operations workflows, SOP gaps, documentation expectations, and manager readiness needs.

2

Design

Mapped role-based learning objectives, weekly milestones, blended learning activities, assessment points, manager reinforcement, and performance support needs.

3

Develop

Created curriculum map, eLearning outline, facilitator guide, learner guide, manager check-ins, job aids, SOP toolkit, knowledge checks, and LMS tracking templates.

4

Implement

Structured the onboarding journey for LMS assignment, manager-led coaching, weekly milestones, guided workflow practice, and readiness check-ins.

5

Evaluate

Designed Kirkpatrick Level 1–4 evaluation using learner feedback, quiz scores, scenario assessments, manager observations, completion data, and operational readiness indicators.

Decision Flow

Exception Handling — Step by Step

Identify the exception type
Verify order status or system record
Review documentation and customer notes
Determine whether the issue is order-related, inventory-related, system-related, customer-related, or compliance-sensitive
Use the escalation matrix if standard steps do not resolve the issue
Communicate next steps clearly to the customer or internal team
Document actions taken, owner, timestamp, and next step
Confirm resolution, handoff, or follow-up date
Common Scenarios

When to use this job aid

  • Delayed order
  • Missing documentation
  • Inventory discrepancy
  • Customer inquiry about order status
  • System access issue
  • Urgent escalation request
  • Incorrect or incomplete customer information

Rule of thumb

Start with standard lookup, documentation review, and SOP-guided troubleshooting. Escalate when the issue cannot be resolved through standard steps, when documentation is incomplete, or when customer impact requires urgent review.

Assessment Strategy

Learning was designed to be measured across confidence, knowledge, behavior, completion, and operational readiness — not just course completion.

Baseline Pre-Check

Measures learner confidence, role familiarity, and prior exposure to digital retail operations workflows.

Module Knowledge Checks

Confirms understanding of order lifecycle, documentation standards, escalation paths, and customer communication expectations.

Scenario-Based Assessment

Tests how learners respond to delayed orders, inventory issues, missing documentation, and customer inquiries.

Manager Observation

Captures on-the-job application through workflow practice, coaching notes, and readiness indicators.

LMS Completion Tracking

Tracks assigned modules, due dates, overdue items, scores, completion status, and check-in milestones.

Final Readiness Review

Confirms whether the learner can perform standard workflows independently and escalate complex issues appropriately.

Design Decisions

Why this program is built to work in a real operations environment.

• Visual UX & Somatic Design System: Leveraged a background in fine arts to custom-build the user interface layout, iconography, and color theory. Rather than relying on generic corporate stock assets, the visual ecosystem was intentionally mapped using warm, grounding tones to lower learner cognitive load, mitigate onboarding anxiety, and optimize long-term knowledge retention.

Blended learning

Combines eLearning, manager coaching, job aids, and workflow practice to reduce overload and reinforce learning over time.

Scenario-based activities

Reflects real decisions employees face in order flow, inventory exceptions, customer communication, and escalation handling.

Performance support

Gives learners job aids and SOP-style tools they can use at the moment of need instead of relying only on memory.

Manager reinforcement

Creates structured 30/60/90 day coaching moments and clearer accountability for readiness.

LMS visibility

Gives stakeholders a dashboard view of completion, overdue learning, knowledge checks, and readiness risk.

Evaluation alignment

Connects learning activity to operational readiness, documentation quality, and performance expectations.

Targeted Learning & Business Outcomes

This independent portfolio case study was designed to support the following targeted outcomes:

  • Faster and more consistent new-hire ramp-up
  • Clearer understanding of digital retail operations order flow
  • Improved confidence handling customer and operations scenarios
  • Better documentation quality and handoff consistency
  • Stronger manager visibility into learner readiness
  • Reduced repeated support questions during onboarding
  • More consistent use of SOPs, job aids, and escalation paths
  • Better alignment between onboarding activities and operational performance expectations
